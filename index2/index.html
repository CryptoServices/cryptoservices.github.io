Vetting protocols and implementations

Cryptography Services has years of practical experience dealing with complex protocol and their implementations. In these last few years we have taken part in the open audit of OpenSSL and Docker, and have worked publicly with the Open Crypto Audit Project (OCAP) to ensure the cryptographic correctness of projects like Truecrypt. Engagements have made us review many different protocols like QUIC, the Signal Protocol, Olm & Megolm, TLS (up to version 1.3) or even the Noise Protocol Framework.

Assessing cryptographic correctness is important. Implementations have to follow specifications to the letter, without diverting and taking wrong turns. High-assurance cryptography has became especially important when it comes to financial cryptography (mobile payments, high frequency trading, distributed ledgers) or privacy enhancing technologies with Tor-like networks and TLS-like protocols.

Advising on Design and Deployement

Cryptography Services' expertise covers the design and deployment of diverse type of products and infrastructures through their different phases: establishment of trust, provisioning of devices, updating schemes, public key infrastructures (PKI), protection of data at rest...

We wrote the Common x509 certificate creation and validation pitfalls guide and conducted the audit of Let's Encrypt as part as of the Internet Security Research Group initiative. We've also written the Tor Browser Hardening Study and are maintaining a hardened fork of OpenVPN currently used by the Dutch Government.

Our work consist of identifying threat vectors and foreseeing the boundaries of what our customers want to protect against. From man-in-the-middle (MITM) attackers able to decrypt communications to physical breaches leading to the extraction of personally identifiable information (PII), we help our clients understand the importance of a solid threat model and where cryptography resides in it. Take a look at our more detailed article on threat modeling and the importance of cryptography.

Reviewing novel and advanced cryptography

Cryptography Services stays up to date, sponsoring events like Real World Crypto or the Underhanded Crypto Contest, and sending its members to the different conferences the field has to offer. With this continual quest of acquiring knowledge, Cryptography Services has obtained experience in numerous area of advanced cryptography. The security of distributed ledgers, smart contracts, the blockchain (check our recent audit of Zcash), whitebox cryptography, light-weight cryptography, database encryption and even post-quantum cryptography are examples of advanced concepts we are knowledgeable in.

Identifying weaknesses in cryptosystems

One part of Cryptography Services' goal is to find what can go wrong in our customers' cryptosystems. We have published research on block ciphers cryptanalysis last year, as well as potential backdoors in Diffie-Hellman earlier this year.

These failures expand beyond the paper, notably in side-channel attacks. Check out our whitepaper on timing attacks against ECDSA. From RSA's blinding techniques to Differential Power Analysis, we've got it covered.

We're also the people behind the CryptoPals and the MicroCorruption online challenges that you might have heard of!
